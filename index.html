THE STORY

Where this started and why it matters.

I'm Wyeth Anzilotti, a senior at Lehigh University studying computer science and human-computer interaction. This project started with a simple observation about my brother Luke.

Luke's mind works differently‚Äîfaster in some ways, slower in others. Watching him interact with technology made something obvious: Technilogy wasnt designed with the human experience in mind. They're designed for a perfect user that doesn't really exist.

"The problem isn't the person. The problem is the interface."

Every notification assumes you want interruption. Every UI assumes linear thinking. Every device punishes divergence. So I started asking: what if we built differently?

CRUMB is my attempt at an answer. A companion device that adapts to you‚Äîyour rhythms, your routines, your way of thinking. Not a replacement for phones or computers, but something gentler. Something that learns instead of demanding.

It's housed in a calculator shell. It runs locally. It doesn't send your data anywhere. And eventually, it could connect with other CRUMBs to form networks without corporate servers‚Äîdistributed computing that serves communities, not companies.

This is early. I'm still figuring out what it should be. But I think the direction matters.

PRINCIPLES

The values that guide development

01

Open Source

Code should be readable and auditable. Every line is available for review, modification, and learning. Transparency builds trust.

02

Privacy by Default

Your data stays on your device. No cloud uploads, no analytics, no tracking. Privacy isn't a feature‚Äîit's the foundation.

03

Mutual respect

If we want computers to respect us, its time we start respecting them. Think of CRUMB as not just another tech device to replace the IPhone, but a living, breathing soul.

04

Built to Last

Replacable components. Repairable design. Documentation for DIY fixes. Technology that grows with you instead of becoming obsolete.

05

Joy in Interaction

Tactile feedback matters. Small delights matter. Technology doesn't have to be cold or efficient to be useful.

06

Community-Driven

This project evolves with input from people who use it. Features are built based on real needs, not market projections.

07

Technology as Living System

CRUMB isn't just software running on hardware‚Äîit's a system that learns, adapts, and evolves. When we treat it as alive, something shifts. We become tenders, not just users. And living things grow faster when they're cared for.
