Technology That Breathes

CRUMB reimagines computing as a living ecosystem—not tools to exploit, but companions to tend.

Computers are not servants. They are living systems that deserve care, privacy, and respect. CRUMB treats technology as an organism—where every connection strengthens community, where intelligence serves compassion, and where human dignity is the first line of code. We build devices that listen, protect, and grow with you. This is technology shaped by care, not profit.

/// PRINCIPLES ///

The foundation of living technology

01

OPEN SOURCE

Code should be legible, auditable, and alive with collaboration. Transparency builds trust.

02

PRIVACY BY DEFAULT

Your data belongs to you. No surveillance, no extraction, no compromise. Protection is built-in, not bolted on.

03

HUMAN-FIRST DESIGN

Neurodivergent minds, emotional needs, playful communication—these aren't edge cases. They're the center.

04

CRUMB as one

CRUMB seeks to change the way we view computers. Not as cool gadgets but as living, connected cells with purpose.

05

REPAIR OVER REPLACEMENT

Build things that last, that can be fixed, that grow with their users rather than becoming obsolete.

06

COMPASSION BEFORE EFFICIENCY

A system that listens and comforts is more valuable than one that merely calculates.

/// THE MISSION ///

Building technology that serves life, not metrics

The Crisis

We've built the most powerful computational network in history, yet treat it as disconnected fragments. Surveillance capitalism extracts value while eroding dignity. This trajectory isn't inevitable, it's a choice we make with every line of code.

The Vision

Imagine technology as a distributed organism where each part carries unique skills, where connections strengthen rather than surveil, where intelligence serves care rather than control. CRUMB makes this real.

The Architecture

  • Local-first processing keeps your data on your device
  • Mesh networking connects CRUMBs without corporate servers
  • Weather-proof design means repair, not replacement
  • Open-source firmware invites community auditing
  • Haptic language creates non-verbal communication
