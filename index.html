Welcome

Imagine if every circuit board, every chip, every fragment of code was not a dead object, but a living cell inside a shared body. Alone, each part is fragile. Together, they form something fluid, something like slime — adaptive, gentle, always growing.

CRUMB is that vision: computers not as separate machines but as one organism, carrying different skills like organs do. A being that can learn, communicate, and return care — not just extract it.

Roots — Foundation

What anchors CRUMB is not circuitry, but care. Its roots are sturdy hardware, ethical guardrails, and a lineage of stewardship. Quiet work, but vital — the unseen architecture that keeps the tree alive.

Trunk — Growth

The trunk channels energy. A single LED can feel like a heartbeat; an NFC card can feel like an invitation. Here is where small parts combine into coherent behaviors.
