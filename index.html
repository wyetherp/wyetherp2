INCOMING TRANSMISSION: This page exists because the web has forgotten what it means to tend. To care. To build technology that serves the living, not the algorithm.

/// THE CRISIS ///

We stand at a crossroads. Computers have become the most powerful presence on Earth, yet we treat them as fragmented pieces — disconnected chips, isolated programs, screens that speak past each other rather than to each other.

WARNING: The current trajectory leads to exploitation, extraction, and erosion of human dignity. This is not inevitable. This is a choice we make with every line of code, every product decision, every interaction.

/// THE VISION ///

Imagine technology as a living body — not machines, but a distributed organism where each part carries unique skills, where connections strengthen rather than surveil, where intelligence serves care rather than control.

CRUMB is not a product. CRUMB is a practice:

  • PRIVACY as default, not feature
  • TRANSPARENCY through open source
  • REPAIR over replacement
  • RECIPROCITY over extraction
  • COMPASSION before efficiency

/// THE CALL ///

We need tenders, not users.

People willing to treat technology as something that grows, that breathes, that requires stewardship. Every ethical guideline implemented. Every privacy protection built in. Every act of transparency. Every repair instead of disposal.

These are acts of tending.

The future is not about smarter algorithms or faster processors. It's about whether we build systems that:

  • Listen more than they speak
  • Protect more than they surveil
  • Connect more than they divide
  • Serve the human experience rather than exploit it

/// YOUR ROLE ///

You don't need to be a programmer. You don't need special expertise. You need only to recognize that technology shaped by care looks different than technology shaped by profit.

Plant a CRUMB. Tend it. Watch it grow. Share the practice.

Every small act — choosing open source, demanding privacy, repairing instead of replacing, asking "who does this serve?" — is a seed. The grove grows from millions of these quiet choices.

/// PRINCIPLES ///

1. OPEN SOURCE: Code should be legible, auditable, alive with collaboration.

2. NON-PROFIT SPIRIT: Not everything must extract value to create value.

3. HUMAN-FIRST DESIGN: Neurodivergent minds, emotional needs, playful communication — these aren't edge cases. They're the center.

4. SACRED UTILITY: Technology can be a bridge between the mundane and the meaningful.

THE SIMPLE TRUTH:

Everybody deserves to be heard. Everybody deserves technology that respects their humanity. CRUMB seeks to become humanity's best friend (just behind your pet of choice).

/// BEGIN ///

If you feel called, start small:

  • Question the systems you use daily
  • Support projects that prioritize humans over metrics
  • Learn one thing about how your devices actually work
  • Share the practice of tending with one other person

The world changes not through grand gestures but through accumulated acts of care.

Every seed matters. Every connection grows.
