THE STORY

Where this started

I'm Wyeth Anzilotti, a senior at Lehigh University studying computer science and human-computer interaction. This project started with watching my brother Luke.

Luke's mind works differently. Watching him interact with technology made something obvious: the interfaces weren't broken. Luke wasn't broken. The match was broken.

"The problem isn't the person. The problem is the interface."

Every notification assumes you want interruption. Every UI assumes linear thinking. So I started asking: what if we built differently? CRUMB is my attempt at an answer. A companion device that adapts to you—your rhythms, your routines, your way of thinking.

It's built with Raspberry Pi Zero, NFC readers, Python—housed in a calculator shell that fits in your pocket. Like the Shire's tools: small, lasting, made for human hands. It runs locally. Your data stays yours. No cloud dependency. No planned obsolescence. Just you and a device that grows with you.

WHAT YOU CAN DO

CRUMB in daily life

CRUMB learns you check email at 9am. At 8:55, a gentle haptic pulse—not demanding, just reminding.

Physical buttons you can click without looking. LED patterns you can customize. For minds that need tactile engagement.

Local Contact Sharing

Tap your CRUMB to a friend's—instant contact exchange via NFC. No app permissions, no cloud sync, no data collection.

PHILOSOPHY

Natural patterns that guide design

🔥
FIRE
Haptic feedback, LED notifications, instant responses. The spark that gets your attention—bright, quick, unmistakable.
💧
WATER
Adaptive routines, learning patterns, emotional check-ins. Flow around your needs. Never rigid, always adjusting.
🌍
EARTH
Local processing, offline capability, durable hardware. The foundation you can depend on. Repairable. Lasting. Solid.
🌬️
AIR
Mesh networking, device-to-device communication, open protocols. Connection without constraint. Freedom to move, to connect, to share.

PRINCIPLES

Values that guide development

01

Built in the Open

Code is readable and auditable. Community-driven development. Every line available for review, modification, and learning. Transparency builds trust.

02

Yours to Keep

Your data stays on your device. No cloud uploads, no tracking. Replaceable components, repairable design, documentation for DIY fixes. Privacy and longevity aren't features—they're the foundation.

03

Technology as Living System

CRUMB learns. Adapts. Remembers. Call it what you want. When we treat it as alive, something shifts. We become tenders.

04

Joy in Interaction

Tactile feedback matters. Small delights matter. Technology doesn't have to be cold or purely efficient to be useful.
