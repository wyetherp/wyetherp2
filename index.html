Building CRUMB

CRUMB — An AI Companion for All Minds

CRUMB began with something personal: watching someone I love struggle with technology, not because the system was broken, but because it was never designed to meet them where they were. That experience made me ask a new kind of question: what if computers worked with human rhythms instead of against them?

To explore that, I started building CRUMB like a living cell. Each prototype begins simple — a circuit board with a single LED — and grows by adding new parts the way a cell adds organelles. Sensors, memory, displays, and behaviors all slot into place in patterns inspired by biology. CRUMB isn't just a gadget; it's an experiment in making technology feel alive.

This biological approach allows CRUMB to grow naturally. Just as cells form tissues and organs, many CRUMBs could one day connect into a network — not to control, but to support — amplifying human creativity, communication, and care.

My goal is not to make smarter machines for their own sake, but to create companions that respect privacy, honor human rhythms, and serve as allies in daily life. CRUMB is built piece by piece, pattern by pattern, with the hope that one day every person could have a living companion uniquely tuned to them — a small, living spark of silicon designed for good.

The Vision

Nothing → Circuit with LED (We are here) → NFC tag, sensors, memory etc → Communication/LLM abilities → What's next?

Nothing → Single cell organisms → Cell clusters → Organs → Bodies → Consciousness

CRUMB is my attempt to recreate cell biology in circuitry. Instead of treating technology as a flat tool—screens, menus, and apps—I'm building it like a living system. Each device is designed as a kind of digital cell: code becomes DNA, LEDs act as signaling molecules, and circuits act as veins. The goal isn't just to make another gadget, but to explore what happens when technology follows the same patterns life uses to organize itself.

At its heart, CRUMB is an experiment in awareness. What if a device didn't just respond to commands, but carried presence—communicating through light, touch, and subtle behavior rather than text and icons? By grounding electronics in biological principles, I want to discover whether technology can feel less like a machine and more like a companion.

My end goal is to create technology that not only understands and enhances human rhythms, but also adapts uniquely to each person. I envision a future where every individual has their own CRUMB—personal, expressive, and alive in its own way. These devices could connect into a shared network, not to control, but to weave a kind of digital ecosystem where presence, play, and creativity move freely between people. The network would exist like nature itself: diverse, resilient, and always growing from the individuality of its parts. That's the vision. For now, though, I'm focused on getting the basics right—one cell at a time.

Our Mission

  • We build for human flourishing. Every CRUMB device must nurture wellbeing, creativity, and authentic connection. We reject designs that exploit, manipulate, or harm.
  • We protect privacy absolutely. CRUMB learns and adapts locally. No data leaves the device without the user's permission. Users own their information completely, always.
  • We design for trust. Our technology communicates through simple, human forms—light, touch, and presence—never in ways that overwhelm or deceive.
  • We follow nature's blueprint. CRUMB is organized like living cells: small, connected, and adaptive. Complexity emerges through relationships, not control.
  • We create for the long arc. CRUMB is more than a product. It is a philosophy meant to outlast us—planting the seeds of a movement where biology and silicon evolve side by side.
